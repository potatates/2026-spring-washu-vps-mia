What is a ritual, practice, or routine in your life that is important for your psychological wellbeing and/or fulfillment? Why?
When I think about “the best in me”, I think it can be approached in two ways, how others perceive me, and how I perceive myself. Normally when we think about how someone or something brings out the best in us we think about what we present on the outside. How nice we are, how competent we are. Though I think our inner perception and other’s perception is often closely related, I want to approach this question in terms of inner perception, how I feel the best. Thinking about times where I think I’ve been the best of myself, I often think of LA, which is a human creation in itself. I think being in LA makes me more aware and awake, and motivated. It also gives me time to think and just be. I think LA is a relaxing place for me, but not relaxing enough for me to completely let go, just enough for me to be able to know what I want to do and gain clarity. I also think it is important to address what I think the best version of myself is: clear, productive, aware, and open. I think oftentimes, my worst selves tend to avoid and neglect (work, tasks, myself). What about LA makes me better? I think a big part of it may actually be the sunshine, which isn’t human made. What is human made are the buildings, and the city planning, how everything on the ground interacts with the sun, and reflect vibrant colors. It always makes me feel better looking at buildings under sunlight, especially during sunset. Another part is probably the food. LA has food from all over the world, food I often miss, growing up on a different continent. But also I think this has to do with my family being there, without my brother there to show me around the different food spots I would not have known where to go. When I was back in LA for thanksgiving, my friend whos also from Beijing and now goes to UCLA stayed with me and I showed her the places my brother showed me, and she told me that was some of the best food she’s had there. So I think it might not just be the city itself, but how I interacted with it, how I approached it, and the memories I have associated.
