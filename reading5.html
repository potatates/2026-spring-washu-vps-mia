I think this was a very interesting and calming film. When the first set of generative collages/split screens came up, I was slightly confused, but as they kept popping up throughout the whole film, between different segments, I began to see them as something purposeful. I think it really ties into Eno and the idea of the film, these segments create a space. Like Eno's music, I think this documentary was really successful with creating that space and immersing the audience into a discussion about creativity. I really liked seeing how organic creativity seemed with Eno, how emotion filled the whole process was for him. I think nowadays a lot of the times we are expected to churn things out with deadlines and rules set into place, and it was really interesting to see how freeing the process was for him, which was further amplified by the media of the film. The film also gave me a slight ADHD feeling, it was kind of jumping all over the place, which was pretty interesting, I think it mirrors my mental space sometimes.
