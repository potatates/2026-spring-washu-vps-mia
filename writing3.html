What is a ritual, practice, or routine in your life that is important for your psychological wellbeing and/or fulfillment? Why?
I cherish the slow and hard way of doing something when it’s repetitive and doesn’t require much thought. I take handwritten notes for classes even though typing them would be faster, because I like the way it feels to write words out, and it also makes me absorb the information better. Writing it out by hand gives me more time to absorb and makes me take more effort to get words on a page, which makes me remember them better. Human fulfillment is often arbitrary, I think. We like to do some things because it was always done that way, however, that doesn’t apply to a lot of other things where we do it the new way. Nonetheless, I think we cherish tradition and repetition; we find fulfillment and comfort in repeating something. I think technologies that support this type of fulfillment has to be slightly redundant somehow, like digital notetaking apps on the ipad, they have to allow you the feeling of taking notes on paper, and not speed up the process.
