What is a ritual, practice, or routine in your life that is important for your psychological wellbeing and/or fulfillment? Why?
How does this ritual or behavior contribute to your life? What aspect of it makes it valuable to you? What can it teach us about the nature or ingredients of human fulfillment more broadly? The first thing, or maybe the only thing that came into mind was my OCD counting. I like to count. I like to count letters in words, number of strokes in letters, and I like to manipulate them in a way so the final number is a number I like. I’m constantly doing this as long as there is a word in front of my face or in my ears or in my head. I don’t ever stop counting, it runs like a background program in my head. Though this is completely not rational, I think it brings me a sense of balance that ceases to exist if I do stop counting. I guess this teaches us that human fulfillment can often be arbitrary and come from within ourselves. Many things bring us comfort because we have conditioned ourselves that way, which I think may be a factor in this counting of mine. One day in middle school, I started counting after reading a book about OCD in English class, and never stopped again for another day in my life. Also, I’m not a very routinely person, I have always insanely struggled with forming even the most basic habits and routines. Everything was always spontaneous and not planned. I think the comfort I assigned to this task is a completely arbitrary value, yet it provides structure to my otherwise often structureless life.
