This article was written in 1996, when we haven’t gotten to the ubiquitous computing phase yet. Reading this 30 years later, I think we are in that phase right now, and so a lot of the problems illustrated seems especially prominent. I really agree with the philosophy of calm technology, I think a lot of designs in maybe the 2000s and 2010s were more flamboyant, while now many designers and developers are trying to cut back and return to simplicity, but a different kind of simplicity from the start of the web. Simplicity used to be less thoughtful and more convenient, like a simple HTML file. And while our technology and designs have developed, our simplicity now is a thoughtful simplicity and minimalism that promotes calmness. I think a lot of brands and websites have been moving in this direction. Another problem/concept they illustrated is the periphery, which really reminds me of UX and flow. Good design often means intuitive design, design that doesn’t need you to think about how things work, you just know intuitively what to do and how to get places without it being overwhelming.
